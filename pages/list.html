Best spots for yummy grub in Phoenix, AZ

  1. Ocean Prime

    A high-end establishment serving steak, seafood, and specialty cocktails in Desert Ridge's "High Street" galleria. If you're willing to spend a pretty penny, this is one of the top options in the valley for a night of fine dining. Their crab cakes are spectacular, and their portion sizes are adequate but never excessive. For an extra bit of fun, check out the "Berries & Bubbles" cocktail, which is poured over dry ice for an entertaining effect.

  2. Humble Bistro

    Staying on High Street, a close runner up for the top spot on this list is the cozy Humble Bistro, which lives up to its name visually with an understated brick-and-barnwood motif, yet pairs it with warm Italian comfort food and a lovely wine selection. House-made pasta and delectably simple sauces will leave you comfortably sated, and if you add a glass of wince you may well need a cab to carry your sleepy ass home! Their take on the classic pasta dish Cacio e Pepe is the best I've ever had, but you can't go wrong with any of their pasta options.

  3. State 48 Brewery

    State 48 - not to be confused with the (also excellent) clothing brand - has a number of valley-wide locations, but for my money the original DTPHX location is the best, with a spacious dining room directly across the street from The Van Buren concert venue on the outskirts of the city. It has a funky rock-inspired theme, excellent burgers, sandwiches and pub grub, and a wide array of local brews of all varieties. My personal pick is the Corned Beef Reuben, but be ready; Their sandwiches are sizeable!

  4. Islands Fine Burgers and Drinks

    A regular staple of my dining-out options in the Valley of the Sun, Islands offers classic American with a polynesian twist; think Red Robin with more pineapple. affordable pricing a large portions make this a good family option, especially if your family would like to share a plate of nachos, as Islands is maybe the best spot to get them.

  5. Top Shelf Mexican Food & Cantina

    Another mid-price family option located in Peoria, Top Shelf is a good spot for a variety of classic Tex-Mex options. Though their food can be a bit on the greasy side, sometimes that's what you're after! Their bar is also adorned with a number of TV's set to available sports programming, though there is a separate dining room if you would like Grandpa to pay attention to the family during dinner. Be forewarned, however - Thursday's are karaoke night, so you won't catch me there, unless I can't resist getting Burrito Fundido take-out!

  6. Chez Nicely

    On second thought... let's not go to Chez Nicely. 'Tis a silly place.
